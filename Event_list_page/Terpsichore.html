Terpsichore

In Greek mythology, Terpsichore, one of the nine muses, is revered for her magnificent dancing. Embodying this spirit, the dance event of Odyssey 2020 is bound to be a challenging, yet memorable one for all those with a passion for dance.

Rounds:

  • Round 1:
    • The participants are required to choreograph and film a routine based on the prompt - ‘interdependence‘. This round serves as a great opportunity to not only display skill, but also creativity in terms of interpreting the prompt.
    • Submissions must be 2 minutes long.
  • Round 2:
    • The qualified teams will have to choreograph another dance routine. Prompts for this round will be assigned through a virtual spin wheel. Rest assured the prompts are anything but commonplace. Furthermore, in order to scale up the difficulty level, the participants will be given only twenty minutes to prepare, film and submit their piece. This event encourages participants to put their best foot forward, and create a mesmerizing performance!
    • Round 2 entries must be approximately 1.5 to 2 minutes long

Rules:

  • Students from grades 9-12 are eligible to participate in this event.
  • This is an individual event, but schools may send 3-4 individual participants.
  • All round one submission must be sent to the official mail id (events@npshsr.com ), by 6 November 2020
  • Props can be used if they help with the performance. However, no additional marks are awarded for props.
