Tainia

American animator John Lasseter once said that “short films have a sense of raw creativity and unbridled joy, that is different from anything you see in a feature film “. Get ready to display your ingenuity by creating an engaging short film for the event Tainia!
All teams will be given a word, taking inspiration from which, they must create a 5-10 minute short film. The participants are free to interpret and depict the word in whatever manner they see fitting. The main aim is to come up with a concept that embodies their vision and connects with the judges. To make the competition truly testing, the participants will receive a surprise element a couple of days before the submission date, that they must incorporate into their film.
All films will first be judged by a round of internal judges. Those that qualify will once again be judged by an external judge

Rules:

  • Students from grades 9-12 are eligible to participate in this event.
  • Every school may send only one team, consisting of 4-7 members(including the editor).
  • All films must have an element of humor. Points are allotted for this.
  • The short films must be approximately 5- 10 minutes long.
  • All entries must be submitted by 10 November on the official email id (events@npshsr.com). The files must be in the mp4 format, titled as school name + event.
  • Keeping the current pandemic in mind, students are not allowed to meet up to shoot the video. The participants can instead edit multiple frames side by side.
